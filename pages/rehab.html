Injury Prevention & Reconstruction Protocols

Evidence-based rehab for your 5 critical areas. Do these consistently. They are not optional.

⚠️
Important: These protocols are based on proven sports physiotherapy principles. If any exercise causes sharp pain (not muscle burn/discomfort), stop immediately. Pain > 3/10 during movement = stop and assess. Discomfort and stretch sensation are normal. Sharp/joint pain is not.

🦴 Lower Back Protocol — McGill Method + Decompression

DAILY ▶
📖
Root Cause: Desk job → prolonged hip flexion → hip flexor tightness → anterior pelvic tilt → lumbar compression → lower back pain. Protocol: strengthen the spinal stabilizers, release hip flexors, and decompress the spine daily.

📊 Lower Back Pain Response Protocol

Pain 0–2/10
Continue as Planned

Normal training. McGill Big 3. Monitor and track.

Pain 3–5/10
Modified Protocol

Skip heavy loaded movements. Do McGill Big 3 + decompression only. Prioritize walking and gentle movement.

Pain 6+/10
Rest + Assess

No training. Only decompression and walking. If persists 3+ days, see physiotherapist.

5x/week ▶
📖
Root Cause: Anterior shoulder dominance (too much pressing, chest/front delt), weak rotator cuff, poor scapular control, tight pec minor. Protocol: strengthen posterior shoulder, external rotators, and serratus anterior. Restore scapular upward rotation.

🎯 Neck Protocol — Forward Head Posture Correction

DAILY ▶
📖
Root Cause: Every inch your head is forward of your spine adds ~10 lbs of stress on the cervical spine. At 2–3 inches forward (common in desk workers), that's 20–30 extra lbs. Protocol: strengthen deep neck flexors, stretch upper traps, and build new postural muscle memory.

🦵 Knee Protocol — Tendon Strengthening + VMO Program

4x/week ▶
📖
Family History Awareness: Knee health is prophylactic here — not treating injury. Protocol focuses on VMO (inner quad) strengthening, tendon load management, and glute activation (weak glutes = knee compensation). Heavy eccentric loading strengthens tendons long-term.

📊 Knee Load Management

Knee-Friendly Exercise Hierarchy
✅ Best for knees: Hip thrust, RDL, deadlift, step-up, glute bridge, leg curl
✅ Safe with form: Goblet squat, box squat, reverse lunge, step-down
⚠️ Progress carefully: Leg press, front squat, Bulgarian split squat
❌ Avoid early: Deep knee flexion with load, leg extension (knee pain)
Knee Pain Response:
0–2/10Continue. Monitor.
3–4/10Reduce load/range. TKE + SAQ only.
5+/10Stop. Walk only. Physio if persists.
💡
Key insight: Knee pain is usually a glute problem. Strengthen your posterior chain and knees fix themselves 80% of the time.
